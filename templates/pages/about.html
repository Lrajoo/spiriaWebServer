We Offer a Reliable Preliminary Prediction

Consisting of three tests: Spiral Drawing Test, Rest Tremor Frequency Test, and Questionnaire Response.

Parkinson's disease is a progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement, with 20,000 reported cases in the US each year. At present, Parkinson’s disease diagnosis requires physical visits and often time involves elaborate tests such as blood tests and imaging tests. A complete test would cost upwards of $4000 and might prove a burden for some. Other available alternatives are smartphone apps that could help with early symptoms detection for Parkinson’s but are not accessible to non-smartphone users such as elderly people. There is a need for a device that can be operated locally without a mobile device for a cheap price. We are formulating a way to detect Parkinson’s in its early stages by making the test as compact and convenient to the general public as possible. We want to create an affordable prototype that enables people to conduct preliminary self-tests to determine if they should go see a doctor to get checked for Parkinson’s. To do this, we will use the spiral-drawing test as proposed by researchers. The test will involve tracing a spiral shape on any flat surface projected by a projector.

We will also do a rest tremor test. Patients will do a count with their arm rested. A tremor of around 4 Hz is suggestive of Parkinson’s. The glove captures the coordinates and transmits via Bluetooth to the microcontroller which is then shared with the Raspberry Pi. The coordinates of the spiral points would be sent to the Google Cloud services by the Raspberry Pi for machine learning analysis.

The user will then be presented with a questionnaire to fill out. The questions will be simple ‘yes’ or ‘no’ and each question is weighted. Finally, after considering the results of the three tests, the device will then suggest if the user should consult a neurologist for further diagnosis.

Vision:

To be able to provide a self-diagnosis option to the public where healthcare is not readily available or affordable.
